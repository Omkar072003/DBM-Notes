What is E-Business?

E-business means doing business activities using the internet, like buying/selling, providing services, and communicating online. It includes everything from internal operations to customer interactions and is broader than just online sales.

What is E-Commerce?

E-commerce refers to the buying and selling of goods/services over the internet, such as shopping on Amazon or ordering food online. It is a part of e-business and mainly focuses on transactions between buyers and sellers.

E-Commerce Threats

  • Phishing – Fake emails/websites tricking users to reveal sensitive information
  • Malware – Harmful software that steals or destroys data
  • Identity Theft – Hackers stealing personal or financial data
  • Hacking – Unauthorized access to systems and websites
  • DoS Attacks – Overloading the website with traffic to disrupt service
  • Credit Card Fraud – Unauthorized use of card information
  • Weak Passwords – Easy-to-guess login credentials compromising accounts
  • Insider Threats – Internal employees misusing access or leaking data
  • Unsecure Website – Lack of HTTPS encryption makes data transfer vulnerable
  • Fake Online Stores – Imitation sites tricking customers into fraud

Encryption

Encryption locks your data so only the right person can unlock it. It scrambles readable data into a coded form that only authorized users can decode using special keys. It’s used in secure websites, messaging apps, and online transactions to protect sensitive information.

Public and Private Key Cryptography

It uses two keys: a public key (shared) and a private key (kept secret). One encrypts, the other decrypts. Commonly used in secure email communication, website security (HTTPS), and blockchain systems. It ensures confidentiality and authentication.

Digital Signatures

A digital signature proves a message is authentic and hasn’t been changed. It’s created with a private key and verified with a public key. It ensures message integrity, authentication, and non-repudiation in digital communications.

Digital Certificates

A digital certificate is like an online ID that proves a website is real and secure. Issued by a trusted Certificate Authority (CA), it confirms the ownership of the public key and builds trust between users and websites through SSL/TLS protocols.

Strategic Management Process in E-Business

  1. Goal Setting – Define the company’s long-term direction and specific objectives
  2. Environmental Analysis – Conduct SWOT and PESTEL to assess market and internal environment
  3. Strategy Formulation – Develop plans based on analysis to achieve competitive advantage
  4. Strategy Implementation – Put the strategy into action with teams, tools, and processes
  5. Strategy Evaluation – Regularly assess outcomes and refine strategies for better performance

Formulating an E-Business Strategy

  • Set goals – Define what success looks like for your business
  • Understand customers – Know their needs, preferences, and behaviors
  • Analyze competitors – Study strengths, weaknesses, and market position
  • Choose tech platforms – Select tools for website, CRM, analytics, and marketing
  • Build digital presence – Create website, social media, SEO, and email campaigns
  • Deliver value – Offer great customer service, fast delivery, and quality content/products
  • Create budget – Allocate funds to tech, marketing, logistics, and salaries
  • Build a team – Hire people for tech, marketing, customer service, logistics
  • Track & improve – Monitor performance and continuously optimize

Internal Environmental Factors

  • Company culture – Shared values, beliefs, and practices
  • Employees and management – Skills, experience, and leadership quality
  • Technology – Level of automation and digital tools used
  • Financial resources – Budget availability and financial planning
  • Brand reputation – Public perception of the brand
  • Product quality – Reliability, durability, and value of offerings
  • Processes – Efficiency and effectiveness of internal workflows
  • Innovation – Ability to create new ideas and implement changes

External Environmental Factors

  • Political – Government policies, taxation, trade regulations
  • Economic – Currency rates, unemployment, market trends
  • Social – Lifestyle changes, education, cultural attitudes
  • Technological – Innovation in AI, automation, internet access
  • Environmental – Sustainable practices, climate change awareness
  • Legal – Consumer protection, copyright, data privacy laws

E-Transition (Digital Transformation)

  1. Identify the need – Spot opportunities or issues requiring digital upgrades
  2. Set goals – Define what the business wants to achieve digitally
  3. Analyze current state – Review existing tech, skills, and processes
  4. Plan digital tools – Choose the right software, hardware, and platforms
  5. Train staff – Upskill employees to use new tools and systems
  6. Implement tech – Deploy solutions and integrate them into the business
  7. Monitor and improve – Evaluate performance and make improvements

E-Business Plan for Online Bookstore

  • Business: Sell books online (paper, eBooks, audiobooks). Focus on wide selection, affordability, and regional language availability.
  • Vision: Make reading accessible and enjoyable for everyone, everywhere.
  • Target: Students, casual readers, competitive exam aspirants, professionals, and children’s book buyers.
  • Revenue: Direct book sales, premium subscriptions (early access, free shipping), affiliate links, ads, and publisher promotions.
  • Tech: Use Shopify or custom website, integrate secure payment gateways, use AI for recommendations, and chatbot for support.
  • Marketing: SEO, Google Ads, influencer collaborations, Instagram/Facebook campaigns, email newsletters, and referral bonuses.
  • Features: Book reviews, real-time tracking, category filters, smart search bar, wish list, and discounts for bulk buys or loyal customers.

Firewall as Security Control

A firewall is like a security guard for your network. It watches all the internet traffic coming in and going out of your system. It blocks harmful data and lets safe information through, just like a filter.

  • Protects against hackers – Stops unauthorized people from accessing your private network.
  • Blocks malicious websites – Prevents users from visiting unsafe or fake websites.
  • Filters content – You can set rules for what data or programs are allowed.
  • Monitors activity – Logs all traffic so administrators can spot suspicious behavior.
  • Types: Software firewalls (installed on computers) and hardware firewalls (dedicated devices between network and internet).

Skills Required to Manage E-Business

  • Technical Skills – Understanding websites, apps, security, and basic coding.
  • Marketing Skills – Knowing how to advertise online using SEO, social media, and email campaigns.
  • Customer Service – Managing feedback, complaints, and helping users online.
  • Financial Management – Handling budgets, pricing, sales tracking, and profits.
  • Logistics and Supply Chain – Managing inventory, shipping, and delivery.
  • Data Analysis – Reading customer data and making smart decisions from it.
  • Leadership and Communication – Leading teams, managing conflicts, and clear communication.

How ERP Acts as an E-Business Backbone

ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) is software that connects all the departments of a business – like sales, inventory, HR, and finance – into one system. It helps everything work together smoothly and efficiently.

  • Centralized System – Everyone uses the same data, so there's no confusion or duplication.
  • Real-Time Info – Managers can make fast decisions using live data from all parts of the business.
  • Better Efficiency – Automates tasks like invoicing, stock updates, and payroll, saving time and effort.
  • Improves Customer Service – Quick order processing and tracking improves the customer experience.
  • Supports Growth – As the business expands, ERP scales easily without needing major changes.
